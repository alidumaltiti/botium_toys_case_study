Botium Toys: Security Audit &
Compliance Assessment

Transforming a high-risk operational environment into a compliant, resilient infrastructure through comprehensive gap analysis and strategic remediation.

Role

Cybersecurity Auditor

Outcome

The Challenge

Botium Toys lacked structured asset management, proper encryption protocols, access controls, and a disaster recovery plan. The organization was exposed to severe regulatory fines and a high likelihood of a successful data breach.

Critical Vulnerabilities Identified

  • PCI DSS non-compliance (Plaintext cardholder data)
  • GDPR risk exposure (No data classification)
  • Weak access control governance (No RBAC or MFA)
  • Zero backup or disaster recovery strategy

Overall Risk Score

8/10
HIGH RISK

Based on likelihood of exploitation and potential business impact.

Assessment Methodology

Risk Assessment

Evaluated asset exposure, classified control gaps by severity, and assigned quantitative risk scores based on industry-standard methodologies.

Controls Evaluation

Assessed Administrative (least privilege), Technical (firewalls, encryption), and Physical (CCTV, locks) security controls.

Compliance Analysis

Conducted deep-dive gap analysis against PCI DSS, GDPR, and SOC 2 to identify regulatory shortfalls and required data protection principles.

Compliance Maturity Scorecard

Function Finding / Observation Remediation Strategy
Identify Asset inventory is incomplete; lack of formal risk management. Implement automated asset discovery; formalize risk assessments.
Protect No encryption for cardholder data; weak access controls. Deploy AES-256 encryption; enforce RBAC & MFA.
Detect No Intrusion Detection System (IDS) or centralized logging. Implement SIEM and network-level IDS.
Respond No Incident Response (IR) plan exists. Draft and test a formal IR plan with designated stakeholders.
Recover No disaster recovery or backup strategy in place. Establish automated, off-site backups & Business Continuity Plan.

Strategic Impact

Identified 12+ critical security gaps and designed a phased, 6-month remediation roadmap. Implementation of these strategic controls is projected to reduce breach likelihood by over 60% and significantly lower regulatory fine exposure.
